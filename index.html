def m_payment(loan, interest, numperiods):
    q = loan * interest

    # Monthly Interest
def m_interest(interest, loan):
    int_month = interest / 12

    q = purchase * tax

    rate = insurance / 12
    return(rate)

    # Monthly Total
def m_total(mPayment, mTax, mInsurance):
    l = [mPayment, mTax, mInsurance]
    summ = sum(map(float,l))
    return(summ)

    # Grand Toal
def grand_total(monthly, nper):
    monthly = float(monthly)
    nper = int(nper)
    total = monthly * nper
    return(total)

def total_interest(grand_total, loan):
    grand = float(grand_total)
    loan = int(loan)
    total = grand - loan

    monthly = float(monthly)

Initial Thoughts
Based off of what we have observed from the summary statistics and visual plots, it is becoming clear that we can expect to get statistically significant evidence to show that the reaction times of the participants differed based on their treatment. It is clear that we have preliminary evidence of a treatment effect.

2-Sided T-Test for Difference in Means Between Treatment Groups

t.test(nodoze$react~nodoze$treatment,
										alternative = 'two.sided')
## 
									##  Welch Two Sample t-test
									## 
									## data:  nodoze$react by nodoze$treatment
									## t = 2.1576, df = 14.072, p-value = 0.04872
									## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
									## 95 percent confidence interval:
									##   0.2494136 77.3505864
									## sample estimates:
									## mean in group 0 mean in group 1 
									##           677.6           638.8

T-Test Results
Since our t-test yielded a p-value of 0.0487, we can reject the null hypothesis that the two groupsâ€™ true means are equal. Therefore, we can conclude that NoDoze does in fact reduce reaction time while driving. We can make casual conclusions based on this assessment because all assumptions of the experiment and data were satisfied along with finding statistically significant results.

Limitations to Experiment
One limitation to the study is that we cannot account for the previous driving history of the drivers. For example, we cannot be sure that one treatment group is composed of drivers that have naturally better reaction times based on sports/hobbies etc. One limitation that affects the internal validity of the experiment could be the car that they are driving during the experiment, or if they were even driving the same car. Obviously people drive different vehicles and are therefore feel more comfortable in some vehicles compared to others. For example, if they were using a particularly large vehicle like a truck, a person who normally drives a smaller car might be affected in their reaction time based on the difference in size of the two vehicles. One thing that I would like to see before making a final reccomendation about NoDoze is how the product effects drivers of middle-to-older ages. In the experiment, the range of ages amongst the participants was 17 to 25. Therefore, we cannot be certain that the natural underlying health effects of aging would not affect the eficacy of NoDoze.
